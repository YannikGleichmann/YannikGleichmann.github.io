### Math
- 2023: **Dr. Phil. Mathematik** at the University of Basel, Switzerland, in the research group numerical analysis of Prof. Dr. Marcus Grote.
  The topic of my thesis was on *Adaptive Spectral Inversion for Inverse Medium Problems*, [https://edoc.unibas.ch/96027/](https://edoc.unibas.ch/96027/), where I developed, improved, and proved convergence of an algorithm to solve (nonlinear) inverse problems or optimization problems. To solve the underlying forward problem, consisting of a partial differential equation, I developed my own 2-dimensional finite element library, entirely written in MATLAB. Vectorized and object-oriented! You can find my library here: [https://github.com/YannikGleichmann/2dFemLib](https://github.com/YannikGleichmann/2dFemLib)

#### Teaching Math
I also like teaching math. Not only did I do this during my Ph.D. by assisting math lectures at the university, I was also part of the project *Wissensbox "Mathematik zum Anfassen"* (Educational Kit "Hands-On Mathematics") from the University of Basel [https://www.unibas.ch/de/Aktuell/News/Uni-Campus/Ein-Rollkoffer-voller-Mathematik.html](https://www.unibas.ch/de/Aktuell/News/Uni-Campus/Ein-Rollkoffer-voller-Mathematik.html). In this project, I showed students from grades 3 to 6 how mathematics appears in everyday life. However, the main focus was on introducing students to geometric shapes, patterns, and tessellations inspired by Roger Penrose using the Penrose puzzle, as well as fractals and where they can be found in nature. I also introduced an ongoing class project in which students built increasingly larger structures from multiple tetrahedra, which I taught them to fold from a single sheet of paper, inspired by the Sierpinski triangle.
